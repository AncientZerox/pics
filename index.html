About

The piezoelectric generator is a device that converts mechanical stress into electrical energy. It works on the principle of piezoelectricity, where certain materials generate an electric charge when subjected to mechanical stress.

How It Works

The piezoelectric generator consists of a piezoelectric material, such as quartz or certain ceramics, which generates an electric charge when compressed or stretched. This charge can be harvested and used to power small electronic devices.

Components

  • Piezoelectric material (e.g., quartz, ceramics)
  • Mechanical stress source (e.g., weight, vibration)
  • Rectifier circuit to convert AC to DC
  • Storage capacitor or battery

Applications

Piezoelectric generators can be used in various applications, including:

  • Energy harvesting from footsteps
  • Powering small sensors and devices
  • Vibration energy harvesting
